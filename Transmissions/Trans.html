Saginaw / SM318 – 3 Speed Manual

The most common light-duty manual in 67–72 trucks. Often column-shift (“3-on-the-tree”) or floor-shift depending on the setup. Great simple cruiser transmission.

Type: Manual Speeds: 3 Common Use: Base trans Shift: Column/Floor Notes: Light duty

SM420 – 4 Speed Manual (Granny Low)

Legendary heavy-duty 4-speed with a super low 1st gear (“granny gear”). Very strong. Common in earlier years before the SM465 took over.

Type: Manual Speeds: 4 1st Gear: ~7.05:1 Shift: Floor Notes: Non-synchro 1st

SM465 – 4 Speed Manual (Granny Low)

The go-to heavy-duty 4-speed for late Action Line trucks. Tough as nails and perfect for work builds, towing, and old-school reliability.

Type: Manual Speeds: 4 1st Gear: ~6.55:1 Years: 1968–1972 Notes: Dual PTO covers

Powerglide – 2 Speed Automatic

Simple, smooth, and durable. Only two gears, so it’s not the fastest off the line, but it’s known for reliability and easy cruising.

Type: Automatic Speeds: 2 1st Gear: ~1.76:1 Notes: Vacuum modulator Use: Light duty

TH350 – 3 Speed Automatic

A major upgrade over Powerglide: 3 gears, better acceleration, and a super popular choice for street builds. Often identified by the kickdown cable.

Type: Automatic Speeds: 3 1st Gear: ~2.52:1 Notes: Kickdown cable Use: Light/Med duty

TH400 – 3 Speed Automatic (Heavy Duty)

The heavy-duty automatic. Strong enough for big power and hauling. More common in heavier trucks than C10 half-tons, but still part of the era lineup.

Type: Automatic Speeds: 3 1st Gear: ~2.48:1 Notes: Electric kickdown Use: Heavy duty
